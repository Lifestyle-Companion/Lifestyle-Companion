LIFESTYLE COMPANION — CLOUD ARCHITECTURE NEXT STEP

WHY THIS IS REQUIRED
Every tester will use their own phone and tablet, and one person may use both. Local browser storage cannot synchronise between devices. A real trial therefore needs secure user accounts and cloud data storage.

PROPOSED ALPHA ARCHITECTURE
- Front end: responsive Progressive Web App first; native iOS packaging later.
- Authentication: email magic link or Apple sign-in.
- Database: hosted PostgreSQL service with row-level security.
- Storage: private user-owned records; no tester can read another tester's data.
- Account model: up to 10 invited trial accounts controlled by Founder Mode.
- Sync model: every change is written to the cloud and reflected on the user's other devices.
- Offline model: local queue with later synchronisation when connectivity returns.
- Audit fields: created_at, updated_at, source_device and deleted_at.

SECURITY PRINCIPLES
- Private by default.
- Row-level access based on authenticated user ID.
- Founder sees trial administration and anonymous usage counts, not private health details.
- No family sharing until a separate consent-based sharing model is built.
- Backups and account deletion must be designed before wider testing.

DECISION NEEDED BEFORE CLOUD BUILD
Choose a backend provider and create the project credentials. Those credentials cannot be invented or safely embedded without the Founder's account and service setup.
